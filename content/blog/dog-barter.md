---
title: "Bartering With a Dog"
date: '2026-04-16'
tags: ["culture"]
---


Last week on my way to work, I met a dog walking by herself. I hadn't planned on 
stopping but she asked if I was interested in making a trade. In exchange for
my sunglasses, she offered me a used hair dryer. It was in good condition to 
be sure, but I had no need for it. I towel dry my hair and my wife was out of town.

OK, Ok, she said, if not the hair dryer, how about this new comb? Everyone needs a comb, no matter how you dry your hair! 

It was a good point, but still a bad trade. So once more I declined and started off. But before I could leave she implored me to consider one final offer, promising that this would be it, no more trades, I'm gone after this, see ya later, 
sayonara. I relented. 

Ok, Ok. In exchange for your sunglasses, I will give you ten thousand dollars cash. 

Now this was getting ridiculous. I mean, where does a dog even get ten thousand dollars? I left without another word.
